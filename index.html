SVN Art Gallery

Curated art experiences shaped with clarity and care.

We bring together original artwork, exhibition programming, and practical services for homes, offices, collectors, and creative communities across Ontario.

Our gallery is designed for people who want art to feel thoughtful, approachable, and personal. From discovering a first statement piece to planning a complete collection, SVN Art Gallery helps visitors explore work with clear guidance and a refined curatorial point of view.

Alongside exhibitions, we support art leasing, private curation, artist showcases, and educational workshops that connect people more closely with contemporary visual culture.

Featured artists

Artists With Distinct Voices

  • Elira Voss

    Elira Voss is a mixed-media artist based in Oslo, known for blending traditional oil techniques with digital overlays. Her work explores the intersection of memory and myth, drawing from Norse folklore and modern urban landscapes.

    Her featured works at SVN Art Gallery are selected for layered texture, quiet symbolism, and cinematic color that rewards slower viewing in both private and public spaces.

  • Mateo Rincon

    Born in Medellin, Colombia, Mateo Rincon's art bursts with color and movement. His style combines street art with neo-expressionist elements, often addressing social justice themes and Latin American identity.

    The gallery presents his work for collectors who want bold statement pieces with rhythm, optimism, and a strong connection to contemporary cultural storytelling.

  • Juniper "Juno" Hale

    Juniper Hale, who works under the name Juno, is a Brooklyn-based sculptor and installation artist. Their creations center around the human form, natural decay, and urban renewal, often using reclaimed materials and soft architectural forms.

    Juno's practice brings a thoughtful physical presence to the collection, offering pieces that feel reflective, tactile, and deeply connected to place.

  • Kazuo Watanabe

    Kazuo Watanabe is a master of contemporary ink painting and calligraphy from Kyoto. His minimalist approach bridges Zen philosophy with modern design aesthetics through disciplined brushwork, balance, and restraint.

    His featured pieces create calm focal points for refined interiors, pairing traditional technique with a precise modern sensibility that suits collectors and design-led spaces.
